# Part 2 词汇

1. 赞成/同意

- The author **takes up/adopts the position that** the universe cannot have any beginning or end, and **states his opinion that** we can never understand the universe using the human ideas of time and space. He argues convincingly that the universe has a unique nature.  
- Many studies have attempted to **assess the significance** of diet in the prevention of cancer.  
- John **lays emphases on** examining the vital first three years of a child's development.  
- These statistics **lend support to** the view that attitudes to the environment are changing fundamentally.  
- Some economists **hold firmly to** the belief that a certain level of unemployment is inevitable.  
- I **go along with the view that** crime and poverty are linked.  
- I **tend to agree that** parents often blame teachers for problems which start within the family.  
H. We **have reason to believe that** .../ **firmly believe**

2. 反对

- **Differences exist/have arisen between** the unions and the management over how to solve the problem.  
- **Contrary to popular belief**, it is not true that games would ruin students' academic life.  
- I **strongly disapprove of smoking**
- Local residents **strenuously objected to** the development places.

3. 利用：

- We hope he will **take advantage of** the opportunities to speak English there.  
- It would be better for us to **exploit** this new technology.  
- My daughter did not **avail** herself of my advice (avail oneself of) use or take advantage of (an opportunity or available resource)利用机会

4. 提出观点

- Jones **puts forward** an interesting theory, **backing up his argument with** thorough data.  
- **Fully accepting the fact that** different learners find different methods useful, Jones **makes the point that** motivation is the key factor in most learners' success.
- Although **this has been widely acknowledged** in general terms for some time, Jones is more precise than most, **stating clearly that** motivation is more important than all other factors, and substantiating this claim with impressive data.

5. 重要的 adj. 

- **essential/crucial/vital/indispensable**
- **tectonic**: (of a change or development very significant or considerable) (变化、发展) 意义重大的, 重要的, 可观的: The last decade has witnessed a tectonic shift in world affairs.

- **momentous**: (of a decision, event, or change) of great importance or significance, especially in its bearing on the future (决定、事件、变化) 重大的，重要的，有深远影响的：A period of **momentous** changes is East-West relations.

- **pivotal**: of crucial improvement in relation to the development or success of something else (对事物的发展成功) 起关键性作用的，起中心作用的: Play a **pivotal** role in the transitional process. 在过渡过程中发挥关键性的作用。

6. 有用的 adj: useful/helpful/practical

- **favorable**: to the advantage of someone or something (对某人或某事) 有利的;

They made a settlement favorable to the unions.
他们达成了一个对工会有利的协议。

Consumers say that when a company responds to a review, it puts the company in a favorable light.
顾客表示当一家公司对一条评论进行回复时，他们也将从中获益。

7. 减少 v. **decrease/reduce/drop/decline/plunge** (不及物动词)  
8. 增加 v. **increase/augment/boost/escalate/ mount** (the cost mounts up)（不及物动词）  
9. 上升v.不及物动词

- soar (the rate continued to soar 急剧上升)
- rise (Land prices had risen.)

10. 下降v.

- dive (The profits dived by 61 percent. 利润剧降  $61\%$  )
- decline (the birth rate continued to decline. 生育率继续下降。)

11. 破坏：

- We are **disturbing** the ecological balance everywhere.  
- Exhaust fumes from millions of vehicles and the burning of fossil fuels are causing irreparable **damage** to our environment.

- Crops fail year after year in some of the poorest parts of the world. This has brought equally **devastating** famines to some regions and equally devastating floods to others.

12. 很多：a sea of /a host of / a number of people (可数); abound vi. / a great amount of (不可数)

13. Adverb  

- Jack was **seriously** injured in a car accident.  
- It's **highly** unlikely the boys will get here on time - they're nearly always late.  
- The children were **terribly** sorry that they couldn't come to the party.  
- It's **vitally** important to revise vocabulary you learn - otherwise you forget it.  
- I love Lucy, but she's **completely** mad.  
- This author's first book was **totally** different to this one.  
- I **strongly** believe that political party is wrong about immigration.  
- I **absolutely** love Chinese food.
- My boss has travelled **widely** in Asia.  
- Prices have risen **sharply** in the last year.
